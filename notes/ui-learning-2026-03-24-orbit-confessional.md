# UI learning note — 2026-03-24 — orbit confessional

## What I tried

Built a tiny single-screen toy with one dominant interaction, one animated stage, and one secondary archive area.

## What worked

- The strongest layout move was splitting the app into three obvious zones:
  - input / framing
  - visual stage
  - recent history
- A silly idea gets better when the interface treats it with full aesthetic seriousness.
- The project benefits from having only one real action: launch the confession.
- The animation types are distinct enough that the toy feels responsive even with very little logic.

## What to keep

- “Stylized ritual interface” is a good pattern for future tiny projects.
- If the interaction is toy-sized, the writing should do some of the emotional heavy lifting.
- A single dramatic visual metaphor is better than three unrelated micro-features.
